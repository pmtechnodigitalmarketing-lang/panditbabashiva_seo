SEO COMPETITOR ANALYSIS (STEP-BY-STEP)
========================================

This document explains how to analyze 10 competitor websites in detail.
The goal is to understand why they rank and how to beat them.

----------------------------------------
STEP 1: SELECT YOUR COMPETITORS
----------------------------------------

Start by identifying 10 competitors.

Go to Google and search your main keyword (example: "astrologer in Florida").

Select the top 10 ranking websites (ignore ads).
These are your real SEO competitors.

Also use tools like Ahrefs or SEMrush:
- Enter your domain
- Go to "Organic Competitors"
- Export top competitors list

Now you have 10 competitor websites to analyze.

----------------------------------------
STEP 2: CREATE A TRACKING SHEET
----------------------------------------

Create a spreadsheet with the following columns:

- Competitor Website URL
- Domain Age
- Total Backlinks
- Referring Domains
- Top Keywords
- Word Count (Main Page)
- Page Speed Score
- Content Quality Score
- Internal Links Count
- Schema Usage
- On-Page SEO Score

You will fill this data for each competitor.

----------------------------------------
STEP 3: ANALYZE DOMAIN AGE
----------------------------------------

Use tools like:
- Whois Lookup
- Ahrefs

Check when the domain was registered.

Older domains usually have more authority.
If competitors are very old, you will need stronger backlinks and content.

----------------------------------------
STEP 4: ANALYZE BACKLINK PROFILE
----------------------------------------

Use Ahrefs or SEMrush.

Check:
- Total backlinks
- Referring domains
- Domain Rating (DR)

Focus more on referring domains than total backlinks.

Now analyze:
- Where are they getting links from?
- Are they using guest posts, directories, or blogs?

Make a list of backlink sources you can target.

----------------------------------------
STEP 5: ANALYZE KEYWORDS
----------------------------------------

Open Ahrefs → Organic Keywords section.

Check:
- Top ranking keywords
- Keyword positions
- Traffic keywords

Identify:
- High traffic keywords
- Low competition keywords (easy wins)
- Keywords where competitor ranks 5–15 (you can outrank them)

Export keyword list and plan content around it.

----------------------------------------
STEP 6: ANALYZE WORD COUNT & CONTENT DEPTH
----------------------------------------

Open competitor pages manually.

Check:
- Word count (use Chrome extensions or SEO tools)
- Content structure
- Topic coverage

Ask:
- Is content short or detailed?
- Do they answer all user questions?
- Are FAQs included?

Goal:
Create content that is better, longer, and more helpful.

----------------------------------------
STEP 7: ANALYZE PAGE SPEED
----------------------------------------

Use:
- Google PageSpeed Insights

Check:
- Mobile speed score
- Desktop speed score

Important factors:
- Image optimization
- Loading time
- Core Web Vitals

If competitors are slow, you can beat them with faster pages.

----------------------------------------
STEP 8: ANALYZE ON-PAGE SEO
----------------------------------------

Check each competitor page:

- Title tag (length and keywords)
- Meta description
- H1, H2, H3 structure
- Keyword placement

Ask:
- Are they using exact keywords?
- Are headings optimized?

Find gaps and improve your on-page SEO.

----------------------------------------
STEP 9: ANALYZE INTERNAL LINKING
----------------------------------------

Use Screaming Frog or manual check.

Look at:
- How many internal links per page
- Which pages they link to

Strong internal linking improves rankings.

Plan:
Add more strategic internal links than competitors.

----------------------------------------
STEP 10: ANALYZE SCHEMA MARKUP
----------------------------------------

Use:
- Google Rich Results Test

Check if competitors use:
- FAQ schema
- Article schema
- Local business schema

If they are not using schema properly,
you can gain advantage by implementing it.

----------------------------------------
STEP 11: ANALYZE CONTENT OPTIMIZATION
----------------------------------------

Use Surfer SEO or manual analysis.

Check:
- Keyword density
- Semantic keywords
- Readability

Also observe:
- Use of images
- Use of videos
- Content formatting

Goal:
Create content that is more engaging and optimized.

----------------------------------------
STEP 12: FIND CONTENT GAPS
----------------------------------------

Now compare all competitors.

Ask:
- What topics are they covering?
- What topics are missing?

Find gaps where:
- No competitor has strong content
- You can rank easily

Create content for those topics.

----------------------------------------
STEP 13: FINAL STRATEGY
----------------------------------------

After analyzing all competitors:

1. Target their best keywords
2. Build backlinks from similar sources
3. Create better content than them
4. Improve page speed and UX
5. Use schema for better visibility
6. Strengthen internal linking

----------------------------------------
FINAL GOAL
----------------------------------------

By doing this competitor analysis:

- You understand why competitors rank
- You find weak points to beat them
- You build a strong SEO strategy
- You increase your chances of ranking faster
